How to Choose the Best Standing Desk Height for 5'8"

When selecting a standing desk as someone who's 5'8", your ideal desk height will typically fall between 42-46 inches when you're standing. However, several factors influence the exact height you'll need for optimal comfort and ergonomics.

Start by considering your arm length and torso proportions. While height provides a general guideline, your individual body proportions matter more for determining the perfect desk height. Stand naturally with your arms at your sides, then bend your elbows to 90 degrees—this position should align with your desk surface when typing.

Monitor placement plays a crucial role in your overall setup. Your screen should be positioned so the top of the monitor sits at or slightly below eye level when you're standing upright. This might require a monitor arm or stand to achieve the proper height relationship between your desk and screen.

Consider your footwear habits throughout the workday. If you typically wear dress shoes with heels or prefer standing barefoot, factor this into your height calculations. A desk with a wide adjustment range gives you flexibility to accommodate different shoe choices.

Think about the transition between sitting and standing. Many 5'8" users find success with desks that can adjust low enough for comfortable sitting (around 28-30 inches) while reaching the optimal standing height. This versatility ensures you can alternate between positions throughout the day.

Weight capacity becomes important if you use multiple monitors, heavy equipment, or tend to lean on your desk. Choose a desk rated for at least 50% more than your actual equipment weight to ensure stability and longevity.

Finally, consider your workspace constraints. Measure your available space carefully, including ceiling height if you're considering a desk converter that sits on top of an existing surface.## Frequently Asked Questions

What is the exact standing desk height for someone who is 5'8"?

For most people who are 5'8", the ideal standing desk height falls between 42-46 inches. However, your exact height depends on your arm length and torso proportions. The best way to determine your perfect height is to stand naturally, bend your elbows to 90 degrees, and measure from the floor to your hands—this measurement represents your ideal desk surface height.

Should I get a fixed-height or adjustable standing desk at 5'8"?

An adjustable desk is almost always the better choice for 5'8" users. Even if you plan to stand most of the time, having the flexibility to adjust height by a few inches can make a significant difference in comfort throughout the day. Additionally, an adjustable desk allows you to alternate between sitting and standing, which is healthier than remaining in any single position all day.

How do I know if my standing desk height is correct?

Your standing desk height is correct when your elbows form a 90-degree angle while typing, your wrists remain straight and neutral, and your shoulders stay relaxed. Your monitor should be positioned so you're looking straight ahead or slightly downward, without tilting your head up or down significantly. If you experience neck, shoulder, or wrist discomfort, your height likely needs adjustment.

Can I use a desk converter instead of a full standing desk at 5'8"?

Desk converters can work well for 5'8" users, but you need to ensure your existing desk height allows the converter to reach the proper standing height. Most converters add 15-20 inches to your existing desk surface, so if your current desk is around 28-30 inches high, a converter should work perfectly for your height range.

What's the difference between single and dual motor standing desks for 5'8" users?

Dual motor systems generally provide better stability, faster height adjustments, and higher weight capacity—all beneficial features regardless of your height. For 5'8" users, dual motors become especially important if you use multiple monitors or heavy equipment, as they ensure smooth operation and reduce wobbling at your optimal standing height.## Conclusion
